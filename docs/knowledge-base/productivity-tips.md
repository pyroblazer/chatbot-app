# Productivity Tips and Techniques

## GTD (Getting Things Done)
GTD is a time management method by David Allen. The core steps are:
1. **Capture** - Collect everything in a trusted inbox (physical or digital)
2. **Clarify** - Process what each item means and what to do about it
3. **Organize** - Put everything where it belongs (projects, next actions, calendar)
4. **Reflect** - Review your lists regularly (weekly review is essential)
5. **Engage** - Simply do the tasks

## Pomodoro Technique
Work in 25-minute focused sprints (one Pomodoro), then take a 5-minute break. After 4 Pomodoros, take a 15-30 minute break. This technique helps with focus, reduces mental fatigue, and makes large tasks less daunting.

**Best tools for Pomodoro:** Forest app, Pomofocus.io, Be Focused (Mac), TomatoTimer.com

## Time Blocking
Schedule specific blocks of time for different types of work. Reserve your peak energy hours (usually morning for most people) for deep work. Group similar tasks together to reduce context switching.

**Example schedule:**
- 9:00-11:00: Deep work (writing, coding, analysis)
- 11:00-12:00: Communication (emails, Slack)
- 14:00-16:00: Meetings and collaboration
- 16:00-17:00: Admin and planning

## Deep Work
Coined by Cal Newport, deep work means focused, undistracted work on cognitively demanding tasks. Tips:
- Eliminate notifications during deep work sessions
- Use website blockers (Freedom, Cold Turkey)
- Work in a dedicated space
- Start with the most important task (eat the frog)

## Habit Formation
The Habit Loop (Charles Duhigg): Cue → Routine → Reward
- Stack new habits onto existing ones (habit stacking)
- Start with the 2-minute rule: make new habits tiny
- Track habits visually (don't break the chain)
- Identity-based habits: "I am a person who exercises" vs "I want to exercise"

## Energy Management
- Sleep 7-9 hours. Poor sleep destroys productivity more than anything else.
- Exercise 3-5 times per week - it directly improves focus and cognitive function
- Limit caffeine to before noon to protect sleep quality
- Single-task; multitasking reduces productivity by 40%

## Weekly Review Process
Every Friday or Sunday:
1. Clear all inboxes to zero
2. Review all active projects
3. Check calendar for the week ahead
4. Update next actions lists
5. Set 3 most important tasks (MITs) for the coming week
