# Education Resources and Learning Techniques

## Effective Learning Methods

### The Feynman Technique
Named after physicist Richard Feynman, this 4-step process:
1. Choose a concept you want to learn
2. Explain it in simple language as if teaching a child
3. Identify gaps in your explanation and go back to the source
4. Simplify and use analogies to make it clearer

This technique reveals true understanding vs. surface-level familiarity.

### Spaced Repetition
Instead of cramming, review information at increasing intervals:
- Day 1: Learn material
- Day 2: First review
- Day 4: Second review
- Day 8: Third review
- Day 16: Fourth review

**Tools**: Anki (free, powerful flashcard app), Quizlet, RemNote

### Active Recall
Testing yourself is more effective than re-reading. Strategies:
- Close your notes and recall key points
- Use flashcards
- Practice problems (especially for math/science)
- Teach the material to someone else

### The Pomodoro Technique for Studying
25 minutes focused study + 5-minute break. After 4 sessions, take a longer 15-30 minute break. Prevents burnout and maintains focus.

## Online Learning Platforms

### Free Resources
- **Khan Academy**: K-12 to university level, completely free
- **MIT OpenCourseWare**: Full MIT courses online, free
- **Coursera** (audit mode): Top university courses, free to audit
- **edX** (audit mode): Harvard, MIT, Berkeley courses
- **YouTube**: Crash Course, 3Blue1Brown (math), Kurzgesagt

### Paid Platforms
- **Udemy**: Practical skills, frequent sales (never pay full price)
- **LinkedIn Learning**: Business and tech skills, often free with library card
- **MasterClass**: Expert instructors, engaging production quality

## Study Habits for Success

### Environment Design
- Dedicated study space (not your bed)
- Phone in another room or use app blockers
- Good lighting - natural light preferred
- Comfortable temperature (slightly cool improves focus)

### Sleep and Memory
Sleep is crucial for memory consolidation. Key facts:
- Learning right before sleep improves retention
- REM sleep processes and consolidates memories
- Sleep deprivation reduces learning ability by up to 40%

### Note-Taking Systems
- **Cornell Notes**: Divide page into notes, cues, and summary sections
- **Mind Mapping**: Visual connections between concepts
- **Zettelkasten**: Linked atomic notes (tools: Obsidian, Roam)
- **Outline Method**: Hierarchical structure with main points and sub-points

## Learning Different Subjects

### Mathematics
- Work through problems, don't just read solutions
- Khan Academy for foundations
- 3Blue1Brown for visual intuition
- Practice daily, even 20 minutes helps

### Programming
- Build projects, not just follow tutorials
- Read other people's code on GitHub
- Pair programming and code reviews
- FreeCodeCamp, The Odin Project (free, project-based)

### Languages
- Duolingo for daily practice habit
- iTalki for conversation practice with natives
- Anki with premade language decks
- Immersion: change phone language, watch shows with subtitles
- Aim for comprehensible input at i+1 level (Stephen Krashen's theory)
