# Travel Destinations and Tips

## Top Destinations in Southeast Asia

### Bali, Indonesia
- **Best time to visit**: April-October (dry season)
- **Must-visit**: Ubud (rice terraces, monkey forest), Seminyak (beaches, nightlife), Uluwatu (cliff temple)
- **Budget**: $30-50/day budget, $80-150/day mid-range
- **Visa**: Visa on arrival for 30 days (many nationalities), free for some

### Thailand
- **Bangkok**: Grand Palace, Chatuchak market, street food on Khao San Road
- **Chiang Mai**: Elephant sanctuaries, night markets, temple tours
- **Islands**: Koh Phi Phi, Koh Samui, Koh Lanta
- **Best time**: November-April
- **Budget**: $25-40/day budget

### Vietnam
- **Hanoi**: Old Quarter, Hoan Kiem Lake, street food
- **Ha Long Bay**: UNESCO World Heritage, boat cruises
- **Hoi An**: Ancient town, lantern festival, tailoring
- **Ho Chi Minh City**: War museum, Ben Thanh market
- **Best time**: February-April (central/south), October-April (north)

## Top Destinations in Europe

### Portugal
- **Lisbon**: Alfama district, tram 28, pasteis de nata, fado music
- **Porto**: Wine cellars, Douro Valley, azulejo tiles
- **Algarve**: Dramatic sea cliffs, stunning beaches
- **Budget**: €60-80/day mid-range

### Croatia
- **Dubrovnik**: Game of Thrones filming location, city walls
- **Split**: Diocletian's Palace, nightlife
- **Plitvice Lakes**: UNESCO World Heritage, stunning waterfalls
- **Best time**: June-September (avoid August crowds)

## Travel Planning Tips

### Before You Go
1. Book flights 2-3 months in advance for best prices
2. Get travel insurance (always - medical evacuation can cost $100K+)
3. Notify your bank of travel dates
4. Download offline maps (Google Maps or Maps.me)
5. Make copies of passport, stored separately

### Budget Travel Hacks
- Use Google Flights with date grid to find cheapest days
- Consider slow travel (stay longer in fewer places)
- Cook some meals, eat where locals eat
- Use local transport instead of taxis
- Travel in shoulder season (just before/after peak)

### Safety Tips
- Register with your country's embassy
- Keep emergency contacts in phone and on paper
- Don't display expensive electronics in crowded areas
- Learn basic phrases in local language
- Trust your instincts

### Packing Essentials
- Microfiber quick-dry towel
- Universal power adapter
- Portable charger/power bank
- Compression bags for clothes
- First aid kit
- Unlocked phone for local SIM
