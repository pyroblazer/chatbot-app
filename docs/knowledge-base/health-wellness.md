# Health & Wellness Knowledge Base

## Nutrition Fundamentals

### Macronutrients
- **Protein**: 4 cal/g. Essential for muscle repair, immune function, enzymes, hormones. Sources: chicken, fish, eggs, legumes, tofu, Greek yogurt. General guideline: 0.8-1.6g per kg bodyweight for active individuals.
- **Carbohydrates**: 4 cal/g. Primary energy source for the brain and muscles. Prefer complex carbs (oats, sweet potato, brown rice, legumes) over refined (white bread, sugary drinks). Fiber (25-38g/day) supports gut health and satiety.
- **Fats**: 9 cal/g. Critical for hormone production, vitamin absorption (A, D, E, K), and brain health. Prioritize unsaturated fats (avocado, olive oil, nuts, fatty fish). Limit saturated fat; avoid trans fats.

### Micronutrients to Monitor
- **Vitamin D**: Most people are deficient. Supports bone health, immune function, mood. Get sun exposure or supplement 1000-4000 IU/day. Test levels with a blood test.
- **Magnesium**: Involved in 300+ enzymatic reactions. Supports sleep, muscle recovery, stress. Sources: leafy greens, pumpkin seeds, dark chocolate, legumes.
- **Iron**: Especially important for women and vegans. Deficiency causes fatigue. Pair plant-based iron with vitamin C for absorption.
- **Omega-3 fatty acids**: EPA and DHA from fatty fish (salmon, sardines, mackerel) or algae-based supplements. Supports heart health, brain function, inflammation reduction.
- **B12**: Only found naturally in animal products. Essential for vegans to supplement.

### Hydration
- Baseline: ~2-3L water per day for most adults. Increase for exercise, hot climates, high-fiber diets.
- Signs of dehydration: dark urine, headache, fatigue, poor concentration.
- Electrolytes (sodium, potassium, magnesium) matter during prolonged exercise or heat.

### Dietary Patterns with Strong Evidence
- **Mediterranean diet**: High in vegetables, fruits, legumes, whole grains, olive oil, fish. Associated with reduced cardiovascular disease, cognitive decline, and all-cause mortality.
- **DASH diet**: Designed to reduce hypertension. Low sodium, high potassium, magnesium, calcium.
- **Whole-food plant-based**: Strong evidence for heart health and weight management when well-planned.
- No single diet is universally optimal - individual factors (genetics, microbiome, lifestyle) matter.

## Fitness & Exercise

### Strength Training Principles
- **Progressive overload**: Gradually increase weight, reps, sets, or frequency over time. The single most important principle for muscle and strength gains.
- **Compound movements**: Prioritize squats, deadlifts, bench press, rows, overhead press. Target multiple muscle groups efficiently.
- **Training frequency**: Each muscle group benefits from 2-3x per week stimulus. Common splits: full body 3x, upper/lower 4x, PPL (push/pull/legs) 6x.
- **Volume**: 10-20 working sets per muscle group per week for hypertrophy. Beginners need less.
- **Rest**: 48-72 hours between training the same muscle group.
- **Rep ranges**: 1-5 reps (strength), 6-12 reps (hypertrophy), 12-20+ reps (endurance/metabolic). All ranges build muscle if taken close to failure.

### Cardiovascular Training
- **Zone 2 cardio**: Low intensity, can hold a conversation (60-70% max HR). Builds aerobic base, mitochondrial density, fat oxidation. 150+ min/week recommended.
- **HIIT (High-Intensity Interval Training)**: Short bursts of max effort followed by rest. Time-efficient. Improves VO2 max. 1-2x per week is sufficient; more can impair recovery.
- **VO2 max**: Strong predictor of longevity. Improve with consistent cardio and HIIT.
- General guideline: 150 min moderate or 75 min vigorous cardio per week (WHO recommendation).

### Recovery
- **Sleep**: The most important recovery tool. 7-9 hours for most adults. Growth hormone peaks during deep sleep.
- **Active recovery**: Light walking, swimming, or yoga on rest days promotes blood flow without adding stress.
- **Nutrition timing**: Protein within a few hours of training supports muscle protein synthesis. Pre-workout carbs improve performance.
- **Deload weeks**: Every 4-8 weeks, reduce training volume by 40-50% to allow full recovery and supercompensation.

## Sleep Optimization

### Sleep Architecture
- Sleep cycles last ~90 minutes. Aim for 4-6 complete cycles per night.
- **NREM Stage 3 (deep sleep)**: Memory consolidation, tissue repair, hormone release. Dominant in first half of night.
- **REM sleep**: Emotional processing, creativity, procedural memory. More REM in second half of night. Alcohol and late eating suppress REM.

### Sleep Hygiene Best Practices
- **Consistent schedule**: Same wake time every day, including weekends. Anchors the circadian rhythm.
- **Light exposure**: Bright light (ideally sunlight) within 30-60 minutes of waking. Avoid bright/blue light 2 hours before bed.
- **Temperature**: Sleep environment 65-68F (18-20C) is optimal for most people.
- **Avoid caffeine**: Half-life ~5-7 hours. Cut off by early afternoon (last coffee by 1-2pm).
- **Wind-down routine**: 30-60 min of low-stimulation activity before bed (reading, light stretching, calm music).
- **Bedroom**: Dark (blackout curtains), quiet (earplugs/white noise if needed), cool, reserved for sleep and sex only.
- **Avoid alcohol**: Fragments sleep architecture even if it helps you fall asleep faster.

### Common Sleep Disorders
- **Insomnia**: Difficulty falling or staying asleep. CBT-I (Cognitive Behavioral Therapy for Insomnia) is first-line treatment, more effective than medication long-term.
- **Sleep apnea**: Airway obstruction during sleep. Symptoms: loud snoring, gasping, daytime fatigue. Requires medical diagnosis; CPAP is most effective treatment.
- **Restless Leg Syndrome**: Uncomfortable urge to move legs. Often linked to iron deficiency or dopamine dysregulation.

## Mental Health & Stress Management

### Evidence-Based Strategies
- **Mindfulness meditation**: Even 10 minutes/day reduces cortisol, improves focus, reduces anxiety and depression symptoms. Apps: Headspace, Calm, Waking Up.
- **Exercise**: As effective as antidepressants for mild-moderate depression in multiple studies. 30 min moderate exercise 3-5x/week.
- **Social connection**: Strong social ties are one of the strongest predictors of longevity and mental wellbeing.
- **Journaling**: Expressive writing reduces stress and improves immune function. Try gratitude journaling or daily reflection.
- **CBT techniques**: Identify cognitive distortions (catastrophizing, all-or-nothing thinking, mind reading) and reframe them.
- **Breathing exercises**: 4-7-8 breathing, box breathing, and physiological sigh (double inhale through nose, long exhale) activate the parasympathetic nervous system.

### Burnout Prevention
- Signs: emotional exhaustion, cynicism, reduced efficacy, chronic fatigue, detachment.
- Prevention: clear work-life boundaries, regular breaks (Pomodoro technique), saying no, hobbies outside of work, vacation.
- Recovery: requires rest, not just a weekend. Often takes weeks to months. Seek professional support.

### When to Seek Professional Help
- Persistent sadness lasting more than 2 weeks
- Inability to function at work or in relationships
- Thoughts of self-harm or suicide (seek immediate help: call 988 in the US)
- Panic attacks or severe anxiety that impairs daily life
- Substance dependence

## Preventive Health

### Routine Health Screenings (General)
- **Blood pressure**: Check every 1-2 years if normal (<120/80 mmHg), more often if elevated.
- **Cholesterol (lipid panel)**: Every 4-6 years starting at 20; more often if risk factors present.
- **Blood glucose/HbA1c**: Every 3 years starting at 45, or earlier if overweight/obese.
- **Colorectal cancer screening**: Starting at 45 (colonoscopy every 10 years or stool tests annually).
- **Skin checks**: Annual dermatologist exam if high sun exposure or family history of melanoma.
- **Dental**: Every 6 months.
- **Eye exam**: Every 1-2 years.

### Key Biomarkers to Track
- HbA1c (blood sugar control), fasting glucose
- LDL, HDL, triglycerides, total cholesterol
- Vitamin D (25-OH), B12, ferritin/iron
- Complete blood count (CBC), comprehensive metabolic panel (CMP)
- Thyroid (TSH) - especially for women and those with fatigue/weight changes
- hs-CRP (inflammation marker)
- Blood pressure, resting heart rate

Note: All health information here is for general educational purposes. Consult a qualified healthcare provider for personal medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment.
